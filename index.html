Introduction

Journalism is not just a profession, it is the foundation of a free society. However, CPJ data shows a worrying trend: eliminating journalists has stopped being a "side effect" of military conflicts.

Today it is a censorship tool. Mercenaries, criminal cartels and government structures use violence to silence voices investigating corruption and crimes.

Project aim:

To explore the "anatomy of silence" through analysis of CPJ data on journalists killed/imprisoned. The aim is to identify patterns between reporting topics, violence and impunity in order to determine the real price of truth in different regions of the world.

Data Methodology

Data comes from the Committee to Protect Journalists (CPJ) and covers the period 2010–2024. Only confirmed cases of journalists killed or imprisoned due to their professional activity are included. Records were cleaned, standardized, and aggregated to ensure consistent comparisons across countries and time.

Visualization Methodology

The website is built using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and D3.js. Data was preprocessed in Python and visualized through interactive charts, including linked views and time-based brushing, to support exploration of geographic patterns, temporal trends, and individual cases.
